Main Terms

Bourbon

By law, bourbon must be made from a grain mixture that is at least 51 percent corn. Additionally, bourbon is always aged in new charred-oak barrels and distilled to no more than 80 percent ABV.

Proof

Measurement of beverage alcohol on a scale, in America, of 200. A 100¬∞ proof spirit contains 50 percent alcohol.

Char/Charring

In order to impart smoky flavors and aromas into whiskey, the inner surface of wood barrels (used for aging) are partially burned.

Neat

A drink served "neat" is a single, unmixed liquor served without being chilled and without any water, ice, or other mixer.

On The Rocks

"On the rocks" refers to liquor poured over ice cubes, and a "rocks drink" is a drink served on the rocks.
